Assessment of Soil Erosion Risk - USLE approach

Project Description

The conservation of water and soil resources is of utmost importance. This is especially the case in countries that are very vulnerable to climate change related phenomena. For instance, countries with an arid to semi-arid climate (such as Tunisia) suffer from a recurring problem of soil erosion that is more exacerbated during rainfall seasons. The present study introduces the Universal Soil Loss Equation (USLE) as a way to quantify soil erosion in the study area, and by extension helps in proposing the appropriate type of adaptation measure (e.g., planting trees, shrubs or construction of ridges, etc.) and the possible locations where we can have the most positive impact with the least cost.

The USLE equation is as follows: A = 2,24 * R * K * L *S * C * P
where
A: Soil loss (ton/ha/year)
R: Index of rainfall agressivity
K: Index of soil erodibility
L: Index of slope length
S: Index of slope intensity
C: Index of existing vegetation and crop type
P: Index of adopted soil and water conservation practices
