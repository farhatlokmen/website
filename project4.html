Urban Temperature Heatmaps (City of Athens) - R

Project Description

Government authorities are increasingly encouraging the development of tools that enable an accurate monitoring of land surface temperature (LST). This is, especially, the case of the city of Athens, which receives millions of visitors per year and is considered to be the historical capital of Europe. This study explores the behaviour of LST and Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) over a period of 10 years. The spatio-temporal variability of both LST and NDVI has been examined based on calculating the average composite of the latter for the following months: January, April, July, and October. Results indicate that LST and NDVI tend to be inversely proportional and that there is no apparent relationship between LST and building fractions. In addition, the temporal variability of LST is characterized by a clear seasonal behaviour, whilst this is not very evident in case of NDVI.
