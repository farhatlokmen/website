Site Suitability Selection - Weighted Cartographic Overlay Approach

Project Description

Ensuring the sustainable land planning in cities and rural areas is central to the economic stability of any country. This is especially the case in wealthy nations, where waste is generated at very high rates. Poorly managed waste is, usually, associated with a sharp decrease of livelihood quality and an increase of environmental vulnerabilities. For instance, the unmanaged or improperly managed waste usually clogs drains in urban areas and by extension increases the risk of infrastructure damage due to flash floods, as well as, reducing tourism revenue. Governments and environmental associations are conscious of the need to keep their communities healthy and of the importance of developing and promoting suitable waste management guidelines.
In this context, municipalities need to devise more holistic approaches to managing waste, where they take into consideration various aspects such as the geographical nature of the proposed site location, the rate of urbanization, and the spatial distribution of population. Such knowledge will allow them to choose the most suitable location, whilst minimising risks and costs both in the short and long-term. Understanding where to establish landfill sites is critical to the prosperity of any city. In this regard, managers tend to use GIS software to help in this decision-making process.
In the present study, we apply a simplified multi-criteria model that allows us to rate the different potential locations for landfill sites based on an agreed-upon weights of significance. Two scenarios were examined and compared. Results indicate that assigning larger weights to the slope and land cover factors will decrease the percentage of areas with a “High to Moderate Suitability” labels by a good margin.
